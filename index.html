Study Overview

This calculator implements the perinatal Social Determinants of Health (SDoH) composite scores developed through a prospective cohort study of 328 preterm infants born at ≤32 weeks' gestation across five Level III/IV NICUs in Greater Cincinnati (2016-2019). The study addresses a critical gap in neonatal care, where only 23% of U.S. NICUs implement standardized SDoH screening despite evidence that social factors profoundly influence neurodevelopmental trajectories.

Scientific Foundation

Development Methodology

Two complementary modeling approaches were developed using integrated supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques:

  • Ridge Regression Model (Supervised): Achieved R²=0.19 for cognitive outcomes, explaining 19% of cognitive, 21% of language, and 11% of motor score variance. Optimized directly for Bayley-III scores at 2 years corrected age.
  • Multiple Correspondence Analysis Model (Unsupervised): Achieved R²=0.16 for cognitive outcomes without reference to specific outcomes, capturing inherent patterns in social risk factors for broader generalizability.

Key Findings

The composite scores substantially outperformed individual social risk factors and existing international models. Low income and lower maternal education emerged as the strongest risk factors, with deficits of 5-10 points for cognitive outcomes and up to 10 points for language outcomes. The convergence of supervised and unsupervised approaches strengthens the identified social risk structure.

Clinical Significance: The 12-27 point differences between high- and low-risk tertiles exceed the established 0.5-0.8 standard deviation range for clinical significance, supporting the tool's utility for risk stratification and resource allocation.

Implementation Guidelines

When to Use This Tool

  • Initial assessment at NICU discharge or first follow-up visit
  • Risk stratification for neurodevelopmental follow-up programs
  • Resource allocation for early intervention services
  • Research studies examining social determinants and health outcomes
  • Quality improvement initiatives focused on health equity
  • Case-mix adjustment for institutional comparisons

Model Selection Guidance

Ridge Model (Supervised): Recommended for clinical and research applications specifically focused on neurodevelopmental outcomes at 2 years, particularly Bayley-III scores. This model may be more interpretable for clinicians familiar with regression-based tools.

MCA Model (Unsupervised): Recommended for applications requiring assessment of broader outcomes beyond Bayley scores, external validation across different populations, or when generalizability is prioritized. This approach captures inherent patterns independent of specific outcomes.

Important Limitations

  • This tool quantifies social risk but should not be used as a standalone predictor of individual outcomes
  • External validation is essential before implementation in populations outside Greater Cincinnati
  • The tool does not capture all relevant social determinants (e.g., maternal mental health, neighborhood factors)
  • Results should be interpreted within comprehensive clinical and social assessment
  • Scores reflect associations, not causal relationships
